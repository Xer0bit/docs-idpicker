1 - Authentication and Account

How parents sign in, register, and recover access to their dashboard

Parents use the IDPicker Parent Panel to monitor their child’s progress, review recommendations, and communicate with advisors. This guide covers how to get your account set up and securely log in.

1. Registering a New Parent Account

If you are new to the platform:

  1. Open the Parent Registration URL.
  2. Fill out the registration form with your Email, Full Name, and a Strong Password.
  3. Check the consent checkbox at the bottom of the form.
  4. Click Register.
  5. The system will tell you to check your email for a verification link.
  6. Open your inbox, click the verification link, and you will be routed automatically back to the platform to log in.

Verification Link Expired? If you clicked the email verification link and it says expired, simply go to the login page, enter your email, and click the Resend Verification button.

2. Setting Up an Invited Account

Often, schools or advisors will create a parent account on your behalf and send you an invitation.

  1. Open your email inbox and find the invitation from IDPicker.
  2. Click the Setup Password link in the email.
  3. The browser will open a secure setup page.
  4. Enter a strong new password that you will remember, and confirm it.
  5. Submit the form, then sign in with your email and new password.

3. Signing In (Click-by-Click)

  1. Open the Parent Login page.
  2. Click the Email field and enter your registered parent email address.
  3. Click the Password field and enter your password.
  4. Click Sign In.
  5. You will land directly on your Parent Dashboard.

If login fails, double-check your password spelling. Passwords are case-sensitive.

4. Resetting a Forgotten Password

If you forget your password, you can easily recover your account securely.

  1. On the login page, click Forgot Password.
  2. Enter your parent email address and click Send Reset Link.
  3. Check your email (and spam folder) for the reset message.
  4. Click the link in the email.
  5. Enter a new password, confirm it, and submit.
  6. Return to the login page and sign in with the new password.

Common Questions

Situation What to do
I try to log in but get a “Parents Only” error You are trying to log into the Parent portal using a Student or Advisor email address. Make sure you use the parent-specific link
I reset my password but it won’t let me submit Your new password is likely too weak. It must meet the minimum length requirements (usually 8+ characters with a mix of numbers/letters)
I stopped receiving emails Check your spam/junk folder and ensure noreply@idpicker.com is whitelisted in your email provider

2 - Dashboard and Linked Overview

How to interpret your main parent dashboard and navigate multiple linked children

Once you are logged in, the Parent Dashboard is your main command center. It provides an at-a-glance summary for every child linked to your account.

1. Understanding Dashboard Blocks

When you land on the dashboard, you will immediately see:

  • Linked Children Cards: A dedicated card for each of your linked students showing their name, avatar, and quick progress percentage.
  • Progress Snapshots: High-level metrics showing how many assessments have been completed across your family.
  • Advisor Activity Indicators: Quick badges showing if you have unread messages from your child’s advisor.
  • Link Student Form: The input box used to request access to a new child’s account.

2. How to Link Your Child

Before the dashboard shows any meaningful data, you must link your parent account to your child’s student account.

  1. On the dashboard, locate the Link Student form.
  2. Enter the exact email address your child used to register their student account.
  3. Click Send Link Request.
  4. What happens next depends on the system configuration:
    • Instant Link: If your email matches records sent by the school, the student card appears immediately on your dashboard.
    • Pending Request: The system sends a notification to your child’s student dashboard. They must click “Approve” before you can see their data.

(For more details on managing this link, see the Linked Student Guide.)

3. Opening a Student Card

To dive into the specific details for one child:

  1. Locate their card on your dashboard.
  2. Click anywhere on the card (or click View Student).
  3. The platform will navigate to the Student Detail Page, where you can see their full assessment results, progress, and recommendations.

Got Multiple Children?

No problem. Simply use the Link Student form again to request access to your second or third child. They will each appear as a distinct, separate card on your dashboard.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of a student card on the parent dashboard with the View Student button visible.

4. Refreshing Stale Data

If you know your child just finished an assessment, but your dashboard still says “Incomplete”:

  1. Look for the circular Refresh icon on your dashboard.
  2. Click it and wait a moment.
  3. The system will bypass caching and pull the latest, live data directly from the servers.

Common Questions

Situation What to do
My dashboard has no student cards at all You need to use the “Link Student” form to connect your account to your child’s account first
I linked my child but their stats are entirely blank They probably have not started their self-assessments yet. Give them a gentle nudge!
The system says “Pending Request” for days Your child has not opened their dashboard to click the “Approve Parent Link” button. Remind them to log in

3 - Parent Forms

How to open, fill in, and submit forms assigned to you as a parent or guardian

Advisors and administrators can assign forms to parents for collecting consent, supplementary information, or progress feedback. This guide walks you through opening, completing, and submitting a form from the parent panel.

1. Finding a Form to Complete

Forms assigned to you appear in two places:

  • Dashboard notification banner — A highlighted card appears when you have a pending form requiring a response.
  • Notifications panel — Click the bell icon in the top menu and look for a notification labelled Form Assigned.
  1. Click the notification or the dashboard banner to open the form directly.
  2. Alternatively, navigate to Forms in the sidebar to see all forms assigned to your account.
  3. Forms with a Pending status badge still require your response. Forms marked Submitted are read-only.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the parent forms list with form names, Pending/Submitted status badges, and any due date labels.

2. Reading the Form Context

Before filling anything in:

  1. Read the Form Title and the Description at the top of the page — the description explains why the form is being collected and what you need to provide.
  2. If the form belongs to a specific programme or application, an Organisation Banner may appear at the top with contextual information.
  3. Note any Due Date shown near the title — forms with deadlines will warn you if you are approaching them.

3. Completing Each Field

Work through the form one question at a time:

Field type How to fill it in
Text / Email Click the input box and type your answer
Number Type a numerical value; the field rejects letters
Long Text Click the textarea and write your full response; supports multiple lines
Dropdown Click the dropdown arrow and select one option from the list
Radio Click the circle next to your chosen single option
Checkbox Click each box that applies; you may select multiple
Date / Time Click the calendar or clock icon and pick from the picker
Rating Click the star or number that represents your rating

Fields marked with a red asterisk * are required — you cannot submit the form until they are completed.

Tip: Use Tab to move between fields quickly. The form saves your draft automatically as you fill it in, so you can leave and return without losing progress.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of a parent form partially filled in showing at least three different field types alongside required markers.

4. Reviewing Before Submitting

Before clicking Submit:

  1. Scroll back through the form to read your answers.
  2. Make sure every required field (*) has a value.
  3. Look for any Validation Error messages highlighted in red — these appear if a field received input in the wrong format (e.g., letters in a number field).

5. Submitting the Form

  1. Click the Submit button at the bottom of the form.
  2. Wait for the green success notification to appear: “Your response has been submitted.”
  3. The form status in your Forms list changes from Pending to Submitted.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the success toast notification after submitting, and the updated status badge on the form.

6. Viewing a Submitted Form

After submission, you can still review your answers:

  1. Go to Forms in the sidebar.
  2. Click any form with a Submitted badge.
  3. The form opens in read-only mode — all your answers are visible but the fields are disabled. You cannot change a submitted response unless the advisor or admin reopens the form for you.

Common Questions

Situation What to do
The form did not load The link may be expired or the form may have been retracted. Contact your advisor to confirm the form is still active
Submit failed with a validation error Scroll up — a red error message will be visible next to the field(s) that have a problem. Fix each one and try submitting again
I cannot edit my answers after submitting Forms lock after submission by design. Ask your advisor to reopen the form if you need to make a correction
I do not see the form in my dashboard Check the Notifications panel first. If it is not there, ask your advisor to confirm the form was assigned to your account

4 - Linked Student

How to connect with your child’s account and understand what data you can access

IDPicker requires explicit linking between a Parent account and a Student account to protect student privacy. This guide explains how to establish that link and what you can (and cannot) see.

1. Requesting a Link by Email

If your child’s school has not automatically linked your accounts, you must initiate the request from your dashboard.

  1. Sign in to your Parent Dashboard.
  2. Locate the Link Student form.
  3. In the input box, carefully type the exact email address your child used to register.
  4. Click Send Link Request.

Depending on the platform settings, you will experience one of two outcomes:

  • Instant Link: The student appears immediately on your dashboard.
  • Pending Link: The request is sent to your child. They must log in to their student dashboard and click “Approve” before you can see any of their data.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the Link Student form with an email address entered and the pending confirmation state visible.

2. Opening the Linked Student Profile

Once a link is established, your child appears as a card on your dashboard.

  1. Click View Student on their card.
  2. The platform opens their specific profile page.
  3. You can now review their progress summaries, recommendation insights, and recent advisor activity.
  4. To see live updates, use the Refresh button on this page.

If you have multiple children on the platform, each has their own dedicated card and profile page.

3. Removing Access (Unlinking)

If an account was linked by mistake or is no longer needed:

  1. Open the linked student’s detail page.
  2. Scroll to the bottom and find the Remove Access (or Unlink) button.
  3. A confirmation prompt will appear. Click to confirm.
  4. You will be returned to your dashboard, and their card will be permanently removed.

4. Understanding Parent Permissions

The Parent Panel is designed for monitoring and support, not authoring.

What You CAN Do:

  • View assessment progress and milestone completions
  • See selected career/university recommendation insights
  • Preview and download their Journey Map
  • Chat with their advisor and see unread message indicators

What You CANNOT Do:

  • Edit their test answers or take assessments for them
  • Modify their profile data (like email or password)
  • See private, restricted journal entries or private notes intended only for the advisor

Situation What to do

5 - Student Detail, Journey Map, and Advisor Chat

How to monitor your child’s progress, export their Journey Map, and communicate with advisors

Once you open a linked student’s profile from your dashboard, you gain access to powerful tracking tools and direct communication lines with their educational team.

1. Reviewing Progress and Insights

At the top of the student detail page, you will see their Basic Profile (name, age, grade). Below this, the page is broken down into insight blocks:

  • Assessment Progress: Quickly see which required psychological or educational assessments your child has completed, and which are still pending.
  • Recommendations Summary: A high-level view of the career paths or universities the AI has suggested for them based on their results.
  • Mentorship Activity: See if your child has been utilizing the AI mentors or checking in with their human advisor.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the student detail page top section showing progress blocks and recommendation summary.

2. Generating the Journey Map

The Journey Map is a comprehensive, exportable report that synthesizes your child’s entire profile into a single document. It is highly useful for college applications or external counseling.

  1. Scroll to the Journey Map section on the detail page.
  2. Select the specific Data Points you want included in the report. You can check or uncheck categories like:
    • Demographics & Education
    • Psychological Traits
    • Career Ambitions
    • Social, Family & Health Metrics
  3. Click Preview to see a structural mockup of the data before committing to the download.
  4. Once you are satisfied with the included data, click Generate & Download Journey Map.
  5. The system will process the file (this takes a few seconds) and open a secure PDF download link in a new tab.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the Journey Map section with several data point checkboxes selected and the Generate & Download button visible.

3. Chatting with the Advisor

If your child’s school has enabled Family Chat, you can communicate directly with their assigned advisor from this page.

  1. Locate the Advisor Chat panel on the student detail page.
  2. A red badge will indicate if you have unread messages.
  3. Click to open the secure chat thread.
  4. You can read past messages, ask questions, and coordinate support strategies directly through the platform.

4. Managing Subscription Plans

If IDPicker is offered to you as a direct-to-consumer product (rather than through a school), you control the billing.

  1. Scroll to the Subscription area.
  2. Review your child’s current active plan tier.
  3. Compare available upgrades (e.g., unlocking full PDF reports or unlimited mentor chats).
  4. Click to initiate the secure stripe/checkout flow to upgrade their account instantly.

Common Questions

Situation What to do
The Journey Map preview is totally empty You must select at least one data point checkbox (like “Education”) before the preview will generate
The Journey map downloaded, but pages are blank Your child may not have completed enough assessments to populate those specific sections yet
I see a high unread count, but no new messages in the Advisor Chat Try refreshing the page. In rare cases, the unread count may reflect messages sent strictly to your child (Student-Advisor thread) rather than the Parent-Advisor thread
