1 - Roles and Permissions

A side-by-side comparison of what each user role can access and do on the IDPicker platform

IDPicker has five distinct user roles, each with a specific set of capabilities. Understanding what each role can and cannot do helps you set up accounts correctly and avoid confusion about access rights.

1. The Five Roles at a Glance

Role Who holds it Primary purpose
Super Admin Platform owner / IT operations staff Full control of technical settings, all organisations, global content, and billing
Advisor Head counselor or primary school contact Manages their student roster, runs forms, distributes resources, chats with families
Sub-Advisor Assistant counselor or specialist staff Operates within a restricted subset of the Advisor’s permissions and student scope
Student The young person using the platform Completes assessments, explores recommendations, chats with their advisor and AI mentor
Parent / Guardian Parent or legal guardian of a student Monitors a linked child’s progress and communicates with the advisor

3. How Sub-Advisor Permissions Work

Sub-Advisors do not have a fixed permission set. Their access is configured entirely by their primary Advisor using the Permission Matrix.

  • An Advisor can grant a Sub-Advisor access to any combination of modules they themselves hold.
  • An Advisor cannot grant a Sub-Advisor more rights than the Advisor has.
  • Sub-Advisors can also be scoped to a specific student group — for example, only the students in “Class of 2026” — so they cannot see any other students even within the same organisation.

Example: An Advisor might create a Sub-Advisor account for an office assistant, giving them access only to Forms and Notifications but no access to Students, Groups, or Support Tickets.

4. How Parent Access Works

Parents have a deliberately limited view to protect student privacy:

  • A parent can only see data for students they are explicitly linked to.
  • The student controls the link — they must approve a parent’s request from their own dashboard.
  • Parents can view progress, recommendations, and Journey Maps, but they cannot edit anything.
  • In schools where Parent Chat is enabled, parents can message the advisor through a secure thread that is separate from the student-advisor channel.

2 - Overview

What IDPicker is, who uses it, and how each role fits together

IDPicker is a career and university guidance platform that helps students discover their strengths, explore academic pathways, and receive AI-powered recommendations — all within a supervised ecosystem connecting students, advisors, and parents.

1. What IDPicker Does

IDPicker guides students through a structured self-discovery and decision-making journey using three core engines:

Engine What it does
Psychometric Assessments Measures personality traits, aptitudes, and interests using validated questionnaires
AI Recommendations Matches universities and career programmes to each student’s unique profile
AI Mentorship Provides on-demand guidance conversations powered by the student’s own assessment data

The platform is used by individual students, school-based advisors managing hundreds of students, parents tracking their child’s progress, and organizational super-admins governing the entire deployment.

2. The Five User Roles

Every account on IDPicker belongs to exactly one role. Each role has its own login panel and access scope.

Role Who they are What they can do
Student The primary end-user Take assessments, view personality profile, get recommendations, use AI mentor, upload documents
Advisor Teacher, counselor, or guidance staff Manage student rosters, assign forms, issue certificates, run family chats, configure sub-advisors
Sub-Advisor Assistant to a primary advisor A scoped-down advisor with access only to the students and modules the primary advisor grants
Parent / Guardian A student’s family member View linked child’s progress, chat with advisor, download Journey Map
Admin Platform operator or school IT lead Configure organizations, manage global assessments and AI prompts, monitor usage and system health

Note: A person can only hold one role per account. If a teacher is also a parent, they need two separate accounts registered with different email addresses.

3. The Student Journey

The typical student experience follows this sequence:

  1. Register — Create an account or accept an advisor’s invitation link.
  2. Complete Assessment — Answer the psychometric questionnaire (usually 15–30 minutes).
  3. Review Personality — Explore the radar chart and trait cards generated from the assessment.
  4. Get Recommendations — Refine the AI shortlist using exam scores or personality mode.
  5. Chat with AI Mentor — Ask the persona-based mentor questions about career paths and university choices.
  6. Search Universities — Browse and filter the university database directly.
  7. Upload Documents — Submit required application documents through the secure document tracker.
  8. Download Report — Export a full recommendations or personality PDF for external use.

4. How Advisors, Students, and Parents Connect

IDPicker is designed as a supervised platform. Relationships between roles are explicit and governed:

  • Advisor → Student: An advisor invites students by email, or students register under the advisor’s organization link. The advisor can see all assigned students’ progress.
  • Student → Parent: The student approves or denies a parent link request. No parent can access a student’s data without the student’s consent.
  • Advisor → Sub-Advisor: The primary advisor creates sub-advisor accounts, assigns specific student groups to them, and controls which modules they can access.
  • Admin → All: Platform admins have global visibility across all organizations for support and governance.

3 - Quick Start

Complete first-run walkthrough from opening IDPicker to getting your first recommendations

This guide is for brand-new users — students, advisors, and parents — who want to get from zero to their first meaningful action in under 15 minutes. Follow the section that matches your role.

1. Opening the Right Login Page

IDPicker has separate login portals for each role. Before anything else, make sure you are on the correct URL.

  1. Open your browser and navigate to the login address provided by your school, organization, or invitation email.
  2. Confirm the page title matches your role (e.g., Student Login, Advisor Login).
  3. If you are unsure of the correct URL, see the Access URLs page.

Tip: Bookmark your login page the first time you use it. There are multiple portal addresses and going to the wrong one will prevent sign-in.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the login page with the browser address bar visible showing the correct URL.

2. Signing In or Creating Your Account

If you already have an account

  1. Enter your registered Email and Password.
  2. Click Sign In.
  3. If your organization supports it, click Continue with Google instead for one-click access.

If you are a new student registering yourself

  1. Click Sign Up on the login page.
  2. Complete the multi-step wizard: name, email, password, interests, and profile.
  3. Check your inbox for the verification email and click the link inside.
  4. Return to the login page and sign in.

If you received an invitation link

  1. Open the invitation email from IDPicker.
  2. Click the Setup Password button or link.
  3. Set a strong password and confirm it.
  4. You are automatically signed in and placed in the correct panel.

3. Student First Run: Assessment → Personality → Recommendations

Step 1 — Complete your assessment

  1. After signing in, you land on the Student Dashboard.
  2. Find the Self Assessment card or click Self Assessment in the sidebar.
  3. Click Start on the available assessment.
  4. Answer every question honestly — there are no wrong answers.
  5. Click Submit when you finish.

Tip: The assessment takes 15–30 minutes. Find a quiet moment to complete it — your first instinct answers are always the most accurate.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the student dashboard with the assessment card highlighted and Start button visible.

Step 2 — Explore your Personality profile

  1. After submission, click Personality in the sidebar.
  2. Review the radar chart showing your 8 personality traits.
  3. Read the Personality Overview card and Quick Stats panel.
  4. Scroll down to explore individual trait cards with action steps.

Step 3 — Get your first Recommendations

  1. Click Recommendations in the sidebar.
  2. Click Refine Results to open the refinement panel.
  3. Choose your input mode:
    • Select Test Scores if you have exam results to enter.
    • Select Personality to use your trait profile directly.
  4. Fill in your data and click Apply All Filters.
  5. Review the ranked list of universities and programmes that match your profile.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the recommendations results page with at least 3 ranked university cards visible after a filter has been applied.

4. Advisor First Run: Dashboard → Student Roster → First Notification

Step 1 — Review your dashboard

  1. Sign in to the Advisor Panel.
  2. Look at the KPI widgets at the top — total students, pending forms, and recent activity.
  3. Check the Assigned Forms block for any forms requiring attention.

Step 2 — View your student roster

  1. Click Students in the sidebar.
  2. Browse your roster — each row shows a student’s name, email, and latest activity signal.
  3. Click any student to open their full profile.

Step 3 — Send your first notification

  1. Click Notifications in the sidebar.
  2. Click Create Notification.
  3. Write a welcome message and set the target to All Students.
  4. Click Send.

Image placeholder: Add screenshot of the advisor dashboard first-load state highlighting the KPI widgets and sidebar.

5. Parent First Run: Link Your Child → View Their Progress

Step 1 — Link your child’s account

  1. Sign in to the Parent Panel.
  2. On the dashboard, find the Link Student form.
  3. Enter the exact email address your child used to register.
  4. Click Send Link Request.
  5. Wait for your child to approve the request from their student dashboard.

Step 2 — Open their profile

  1. Once linked, their card appears on your dashboard.
  2. Click View Student to open their full detail page.
  3. Review their assessment progress, recommendations summary, and advisor activity.
