IDPicker is a career and university guidance platform that helps students discover their strengths, explore academic pathways, and receive AI-powered recommendations — all within a supervised ecosystem connecting students, advisors, and parents.

---

## 1. What IDPicker Does

IDPicker guides students through a structured self-discovery and decision-making journey using three core engines:

| Engine | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Psychometric Assessments** | Measures personality traits, aptitudes, and interests using validated questionnaires |
| **AI Recommendations** | Matches universities and career programmes to each student's unique profile |
| **AI Mentorship** | Provides on-demand guidance conversations powered by the student's own assessment data |

The platform is used by individual students, school-based advisors managing hundreds of students, parents tracking their child's progress, and organizational super-admins governing the entire deployment.

## 2. The Five User Roles

Every account on IDPicker belongs to exactly one role. Each role has its own login panel and access scope.

| Role | Who they are | What they can do |
|---|---|---|
| **Student** | The primary end-user | Take assessments, view personality profile, get recommendations, use AI mentor, upload documents |
| **Advisor** | Teacher, counselor, or guidance staff | Manage student rosters, assign forms, issue certificates, run family chats, configure sub-advisors |
| **Sub-Advisor** | Assistant to a primary advisor | A scoped-down advisor with access only to the students and modules the primary advisor grants |
| **Parent / Guardian** | A student's family member | View linked child's progress, chat with advisor, download Journey Map |
| **Admin** | Platform operator or school IT lead | Configure organizations, manage global assessments and AI prompts, monitor usage and system health |

> **Note:** A person can only hold one role per account. If a teacher is also a parent, they need two separate accounts registered with different email addresses.

## 3. The Student Journey

The typical student experience follows this sequence:

1. **Register** — Create an account or accept an advisor's invitation link.
2. **Complete Assessment** — Answer the psychometric questionnaire (usually 15–30 minutes).
3. **Review Personality** — Explore the radar chart and trait cards generated from the assessment.
4. **Get Recommendations** — Refine the AI shortlist using exam scores or personality mode.
5. **Chat with AI Mentor** — Ask the persona-based mentor questions about career paths and university choices.
6. **Search Universities** — Browse and filter the university database directly.
7. **Upload Documents** — Submit required application documents through the secure document tracker.
8. **Download Report** — Export a full recommendations or personality PDF for external use.

## 4. How Advisors, Students, and Parents Connect

IDPicker is designed as a **supervised platform**. Relationships between roles are explicit and governed:

- **Advisor → Student:** An advisor invites students by email, or students register under the advisor's organization link. The advisor can see all assigned students' progress.
- **Student → Parent:** The student approves or denies a parent link request. No parent can access a student's data without the student's consent.
- **Advisor → Sub-Advisor:** The primary advisor creates sub-advisor accounts, assigns specific student groups to them, and controls which modules they can access.
- **Admin → All:** Platform admins have global visibility across all organizations for support and governance.

## 5. Two Types of Student Accounts

| Type | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Organization Student** | Registered under a school or agency. The advisor manages their account and progress is visible to the organization. |
| **Independent Student** | Registered directly (not through an organization). No advisor oversight. Subscription-based features apply directly. |
