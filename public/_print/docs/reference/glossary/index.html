Glossary

Definitions for IDPicker-specific terms, Turkish university exam codes, and platform concepts

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the IDPicker documentation. When a term in a guide is unclear, look it up here first.

A

AI Mentor One of the AI-powered conversational agents available to students. Each AI Mentor has a distinct persona and area of expertise (e.g., Career Guidance, Psychological Support, University Selection). Students choose a mentor to chat with inside the AI Mentorship module.

Application Documents A structured collection of files (transcripts, passports, language certificates, reference letters) that a student uploads as part of a university or programme application. Advisors review and verify each file.

B

Bulk Import The process of uploading an Excel (.xlsx) file containing multiple student records to add them all to the platform at once. The required columns are email, first_name, and last_name.

C

Certificate A formal recognition document that an advisor issues to a student from within the platform. Certificates can be downloaded as PDFs and typically record completion of a programme, assessment, or milestone.

D

DİL (Dil Puanı) A score type used by the Turkish university entrance examination system (YKS). DİL scores come from the Foreign Language Test (YDT) and are used for applications to foreign language programmes.

E

EA (Eşit Ağırlık) A composite YKS score type that combines Verbal and Quantitative skills equally. EA scores are required by fields such as Law, Economics, and some Management programmes.

F

Family Chat A real-time messaging interface that connects three parties — a student, their advisor, and their linked parent — in a single shared thread. The parent must be linked to the student before the family thread is available.

Family Thread See Family Chat. Specifically refers to the multi-participant message thread that includes the student, advisor, and parent simultaneously, as distinct from the private student-advisor thread or the private parent-advisor thread.

G

Groups Organisational labels that an advisor creates to segment their student roster. Forms, resources, and notifications can be targeted at a specific group rather than at every individual student separately.

I

Independent Student A student who registers directly on IDPicker without being invited by a school or organisation. Independent students access platform features using a personal subscription plan and are not attached to an advisor by default.

J

Journey Map A comprehensive exportable report that aggregates a student’s full IDPicker profile into a single document. It is generated on demand and can include demographics, personality traits, career ambitions, and social/health indicators.

O

Organisation Student A student who was invited to IDPicker by an advisor or administrator from a school or organisation. Organisation students are automatically linked to that advisor’s roster and typically access the platform without paying directly.

P

Pass Counter A metric on some assessment modules that tracks how many times a student has attempted or passed through a test gate. Advisors can view pass counts to gauge student engagement.

Personality Tiers The categorisation system used to rank a student’s psychological trait scores:

  • Strong Areas — Traits where the student’s score places them in the top percentile band.
  • Balanced Areas — Traits where scores fall in the mid-range, indicating solid but not exceptional development.
  • Growth Areas — Traits where scores indicate the most room for development and coaching.

Prompt (AI Prompt) An instruction block sent to the underlying AI model that controls how the AI Mentor behaves, what tone it uses, and what kind of advice it gives. Admins manage global prompts; advisors may manage organisation-level prompts if granted permission.

R

Refinement Panel An interface within the Recommendations module that allows a student to apply additional filters (by country, sector, score type, or interest tag) to narrow down or re-rank their AI-generated university or career suggestions.

Roles See the Roles and Permissions page for a full breakdown.

S

SAY (Sayısal Puan) A YKS score type derived primarily from the Mathematics and Science Test (TYT + AYT). SAY scores are required for applications to Engineering, Medicine, and natural sciences programmes.

Score Palette A visual display component that renders a student’s exam scores (SAY, EA, SÖZ, DİL) side by side, making it easy for students and advisors to compare performance across score types at a glance.

SÖZ (Sözel Puan) A YKS score type derived primarily from the Verbal (Turkish Language and Literature, History, Geography) portions of the AYT exam. SÖZ scores are used for applications to programmes in Humanities, Education, and Social Sciences.

Sub-Advisor An assistant counselor account created and managed by a primary Advisor. Sub-advisors have a restricted permission set and may be scoped to a specific student group. See Sub-Advisor Management.

Subscription An IDPicker payment plan that unlocks premium features for a student or organisation. Subscription tiers vary by the level of access granted (e.g., number of AI mentor chats, number of full PDF reports).

T

Token Usage The count of AI API input and output tokens consumed by the AI Mentor and report generation features on the platform. Super Admins monitor token usage from the dashboard to control costs and detect anomalies.

U

University Search A module in the student panel that allows students to browse and filter universities by country, programme type, language of instruction, and exam score requirements. Search results can be saved to a shortlist.

Y

YKS (Yükseköğretim Kurumları Sınavı) The Turkish national university entrance examination system. YKS consists of two stages: the TYT (Temel Yeterlilik Testi) and the AYT (Alan Yeterlilik Testi). The results are converted into four score types: SAY, EA, SÖZ, and DİL, each used for different programme categories.
